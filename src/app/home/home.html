Rare Disease Registry

Welcome to the national Rare Disease Registry (RaDaR). In partnership with Rare Disease Groups (RDGs), RaDaR facilitates translational and epidemiological research into rare diseases through a comprehensive clinical database. RaDaR is managed by the UK Renal Registry on behalf of the UK Kidney Association.

Recruitment to RaDaR is open to all UK hospitals. If your Trust would like to take part please contact nbn-tr.radar@nhs.net. If your Trust is already taking part and you require access to RaDaR please contact your research team.

To find information about RaDaR and rare renal conditions, please visit RareRenal.org.

For specific information about patient eligibility and current consent documents, please visit Recruitment Resources

It is a requirement, where possible, that the renal unit system is flagged to send data. This enables the blood results and medications that are on the renal unit system to feed into RaDaR, which considerably enhances the dataset and avoids manual data entry.

PatientView will no longer be available for patients to access their results after 31 December 2022. It is strongly recommended that all RaDaR patients are registered on Patient Knows Best and the renal unit system is flagged to send data to Patient Knows Best.

It is strongly recommended that all RaDaR patients are put on to PatientView and the renal unit system is flagged to send data to PatientView. This enables the blood results and medications that are on PatientView to feed into RaDaR, which considerably enhances the dataset and avoids manual data entry.

For data entry instructions refer to the RaDaR Recruitment Guide.

For technical support please contact nbn-tr.radar@nhs.net.
